Technical requirements

A stable IP connection at the hotel TV

TV channels over IP require a reliable network connection in every room, directly at or close to the hotel TV.

LAN connection at the TV

Ideally, a working LAN connection is available directly behind or next to the hotel TV.

Stable hotel network

Switches, VLANs, bandwidth and network design must support reliable TV operation.

Compatible hotel TVs

Model, age and technical compatibility of existing devices are checked in advance.

No LAN in the room?

IP over Coax uses the existing TV cabling

If a coax cable already reaches the hotel room, it can often be reused as a modern IP connection. This avoids new LAN cabling, dust, noise and extended room downtime.

  • Reuse existing coax cabling intelligently
  • Significantly less construction work
  • Fast rollout during hotel operation
  • Suitable for IPTV, casting and other IP services

Frequently asked questions

Short answers

The most important questions answered briefly.

What is the difference between Basic, Essential and Max?

The packages differ both in the number of channels and in the included language and country groups.

Can I search for specific channels?

Yes. The channel finder filters by package, language, country, category, HD/SD and channel name.

Does every room need a LAN connection?

Yes. For TV channels over IP, a working IP connection at the TV is required. If there is no LAN, IP over Coax can often be a smart alternative.

Can casting be integrated?

Yes. Casting/BYOD can be integrated as a valuable extension.

Can channels and languages be customized?

Yes. Depending on the guest profile, priorities, packages and additional channels can be defined together.
